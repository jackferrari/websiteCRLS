Hobbies

Sailing


One of my favorite hobbies is sailing. I learned how to sail in Gloucester when I was in the 5th grade. Ever since then I have loved sailing and taken advantage of every chance I can to go out. While originally I only sailed in Gloucester at the YMCA summer camp, Today I mainly sail on the Charles River at Community Boating. When I got to high school, I joined the sailing team and since then I have risen through the ranks to now become Captain. This spring is my last season sailing for CRLS, but I plan to sail at college if the school I choose has a sailing team.

Visit Community Boating's website!

Video Games

Another hobby that I have put many hours into are video games. I started playing games when I was in Kindergarten, since then video games have had a big impact on my life. I first started playing on a Gamecube, but today I mainly play games on the computer. My favorite series of games would be the Civilization series. I love the way that those games combine historical influences to create a tactical game about how humans developed their nations. As someone who loved both history and video games, this game series was a match made in heaven for me.

Visit Civilization's website!

Favorite Food

My favorite food is Risotto. The dish itself is creamy rice with some kind of other vegetable mixed in. I like it with either peas of butter nut squash. My favorite version of the dish is also the version that my dad makes. In my family we make the dish about once a week. It is then used as a possible dinner for the rest of the week. During the winter on cold days, Risotto is one of the best dinners that I have. It is also a really satisfying thing to eat. So many good foods often leave you kind of wanting more, or missing the food. But Risotto fills you up and is just all around enjoyable to eat.

get a recipe for Risotto!

Favorite Place

One of my favorite places to go is Gloucester MA. The city is the oldest active fishing port in the US, and is also home to the fisherman's memorial, which documents every fisherman who has died at sea since the 1600s. The beaches there are some of the best in Massachusetts, and my personal favorite. When I was in the 5th grade my family bought a beach house there, and since then we have spent summers up on Cape Ann. The house itself is very small, and has in the past lacked some of the amenities of our house in cambridge, but it is still an awesome place to spend the summer. It was during one summer spent in Gloucester that I learned how to sail, and I still sail today.
