Northeastern Overview

Northeastern University was founded in 1898, with the main campus located in Boston near the Museum of Fine Arts. However at any given time about a third of the student body is out on co-op. The co-op system of Northeastern is what the school is most well known for. The program will basically have students working in a company for a semester, so that they can get real world experience in the field that the student is studying. This co-op program helps explain Northeastern's 92% job placement rateOn top of that the school also has a very good study abroad program. They try to make meaningful study abroad options for many different areas of study. Finally in terms of academics Northeastern has a fairly unique system of combined majors, where you are able to combine two seemingly unrelated topics that you are interested in. For example, Computer-Science and History.

My Plans at Northeastern

There are several things that draw me to Northeastern. For one thing, I really like the program of co-ops. I think it will be good to see some of the possible routes I can take for employment. If I went to Northeastern, I would probably do a combined major of Computer-Science and History. Another draw for me to Northeastern is the location. I would be fairly close to home, and I would not have to travel very far when I want to visit home. Being placed in boston also gives me access to the charles river. The river would give me the ability to keep sailing. Northeastern has a sailing team, and I would be close enough to other boathouses that I have used in the past.
